Our world is changing at an exponential rate

The population of our cities is rising so quickly that traditional city and social planning isn’t sufficiently predictive, responsive and cost-effective.

Our cities inability to adapt and act, the resulting growing social inequality, strained services and infrastructure, and decline in our natural capital, are threatening our communities’ ability to thrive. The stakes are becoming very high.

There will be winners and losers

We live in a golden age of technology: sophisticated sensor devices and enormous computing power are available inexpensively to even small organisations, and techniques like machine learning can now help humans draw helpful insights from data even in the midst of chaotic change.

The emerging Smart Cities field promises to apply these new tools and technologies to help our communities address their key issues. Success for cities will depend on how they apply these technologies and how they can evolve traditional operating models to leverage the insights they gain to make more informed and more timely decisions.

Standing still amidst the wave of technology innovation is no longer an option.

So what does the future hold?

For those who embrace change we see an exciting path ahead enabling cities, agencies and vendors to collaborate around data with less expense and risk. Partners will win by exchanging data and learnings easily, and by federating them with other organisations.

This exchange between agencies within a city will enable elevated thinking and collective wisdom, and that same open sharing and communication between cities will let us all tackle our enormous challenges and opportunities together as a broader national community.

Our mission

When cities of all sizes can plan effectively and learn from each other, with the cost-effective involvement of commercial vendors, we will have enabled a Smart Nation: this is our mission.

Our vision for our customers is that when the BucketLabs Platform is fully integrated into their operations, we will provide them a new “operating system” where decisions are always made with a complete understanding of the context around the decision.

On this foundation, with an ecosystem of trusted technology partners, more open and transparent collaboration between public and private interests will lower the risk of applying new technology for cities purpose and usher in new ‘ways of working’ that are more flexible and adaptable to the current pace of innovation.
